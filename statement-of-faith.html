Our Doctrine

Statement of Faith

The foundational truths we hold and teach

The Scriptures

We believe the Bible is the inspired, infallible, and authoritative Word of God. It is the supreme source of truth for Christian beliefs and living. All Scripture is God-breathed and useful for teaching, rebuking, correcting, and training in righteousness.

2 Timothy 3:16-17; 2 Peter 1:20-21

The Godhead

We believe in one God, eternally existing in three persons: Father, Son, and Holy Spirit. These three are co-equal, co-eternal, and are one God. The Trinity is the foundation of our faith and the basis for all of Christian theology.

Matthew 28:19; 2 Corinthians 13:14; John 1:1-14

The Lord Jesus Christ

We believe in the deity of the Lord Jesus Christ. He is God manifest in the flesh, born of a virgin, who lived a sinless life, performed miracles, shed His blood on the cross as a substitutionary atonement for sin, was bodily resurrected, ascended to heaven, and will return in power and glory.

John 1:1-3; Philippians 2:5-11; Hebrews 1:1-4

Salvation

We believe that salvation is a free gift from God, received through repentance toward God and faith in the Lord Jesus Christ. Every person who truly repents and believes in Christ is regenerated by the Holy Spirit and becomes a child of God. Salvation cannot be earned through works — it is by grace alone, through faith alone, in Christ alone.

Ephesians 2:8-9; Romans 10:9-10; John 3:16

The Holy Spirit

We believe in the present-day ministry of the Holy Spirit. The Holy Spirit indwells every believer from the moment of salvation and empowers them for godly living and service. We also believe in the baptism of the Holy Spirit as a distinct experience that empowers believers for witness, with the evidence of speaking in other tongues and the operation of spiritual gifts.

Acts 1:8; Acts 2:4; 1 Corinthians 12:1-11

Water Baptism

We believe that water baptism by full immersion is commanded by Scripture for all who have repented and believed in Christ. It is an outward testimony of an inward work — a declaration of death to the old life and resurrection to new life in Christ Jesus.

Matthew 28:19; Acts 2:38; Romans 6:3-4

The Church

We believe the Church is the body of Christ, composed of all born-again believers. The local church is the visible expression of this body, where believers gather for worship, fellowship, teaching, and the breaking of bread. We believe in the importance of being committed to a local church community.

Ephesians 1:22-23; Hebrews 10:24-25; Acts 2:42-47

The Second Coming

We believe in the personal, visible, and bodily return of the Lord Jesus Christ. His coming is imminent and will be the blessed hope of the Church. He will return to judge the living and the dead and to establish His eternal kingdom.

1 Thessalonians 4:16-17; Titus 2:13; Revelation 22:20

Communion

We believe in the regular observance of the Lord's Supper (Communion) as a memorial of Christ's death and a proclamation of His coming return. The bread represents His body broken for us, and the cup represents His blood shed for the forgiveness of sins.

1 Corinthians 11:23-26; Luke 22:19-20

Tithes & Offerings

We believe in the biblical principle of tithing and generous giving. The tithe (10% of income) is the Lord's and is to be brought to the local church. Beyond the tithe, we are called to give offerings cheerfully and generously as God leads, trusting in His faithfulness to provide.

Malachi 3:10; 2 Corinthians 9:6-7; Proverbs 3:9-10
